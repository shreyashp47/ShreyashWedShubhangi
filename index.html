Our Journey: From Strangers to Soulmates

Our story began at a casual gathering, where we exchanged simple pleasantries about our lives. With our engagement on the horizon, we took the first steps in getting to know each other better. As we shared contact numbers, our conversations deepened, revealing our interests and work experiences. It was a spontaneous movie date that sparked a newfound closeness between us. Exploring Nanded together, from visiting temples to sharing meals, we found joy in each other's company. These moments of togetherness brought clarity and strengthened our bond. As our relationship blossomed, we found comfort and understanding in each other, sharing our thoughts and feelings openly. Despite the distance, our connection continues to grow stronger with each passing day, built on a foundation of mutual respect and empathy. Our journey together is a testament to the power of love and the beauty of discovering it in the simplest of moments.

Events

18th April

Haldi 05PM-08PM

The haldi ceremony is usually held separately for the bride and the groom. This one is for the bride. It is a low-key event that involves the application of turmeric paste on the hands, legs and face of the bride. The groom is not allowed to see the bride after this ritual until the final wedding ceremony starts.

19th April

Mehndi 1PM - 5PM

It's one of the oldest traditional Indian pre wedding ceremony where the bride and groom attend the event together and a professional mehndi artist applies mehndi to the bride's hands and feet. This will be having a celebratory festival feel to it with people wearing vibrant clothes and dancing, singing, playing games and a lot more.

22th April

Saptapadi 10AM-11AM

In our Maharashtrian wedding, Saptapadi is the pinnacle. Amidst joyous dhol beats, we circle the sacred fire, taking seven steps together. Each step binds us in love and commitment. As we complete the ritual, we're pronounced husband and wife, embarking on a journey of unity and devotion.

22th April

Wedding 5PM-10PM

This is the main event in the entire wedding when the bride and the groom tie the knot. In the Maharashtrian tradition, the ceremony is marked by the exchange of vows and blessings amidst the presence of family and friends. The groom arrives with the baraat and is welcomed by the bride's side with joyous dhol beats. As the couple exchanges vows, they are blessed by the elders, symbolizing their support and well-wishes for the newlyweds. This union is a celebration of love, unity, and the beginning of a new journey together.
